John McCarthy: The Father of Artificial Intelligence

Exploring the life and legacy of the man who gave AI its name and its foundation.

Early Life and Education

John McCarthy was born on September 4, 1927. He was a mathematics prodigy, teaching himself college-level math from textbooks. This allowed him to enter Caltech at a young age, and he later earned his Ph.D. from Princeton University.

Coining the Term "Artificial Intelligence"

In 1955, McCarthy proposed a summer workshop at Dartmouth College. He called it the "Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence," and with that, he gave the entire field its name. This event is considered the official birth of AI as an academic discipline.

The Lisp Programming Language

McCarthy created Lisp in 1958 specifically for AI research. Lisp was designed for symbolic computing and pioneered important concepts like dynamic typing and automatic memory management (garbage collection). It became the primary language for AI research for many years.

Influence on Modern AI

McCarthy's work continues to influence modern AI through his ideas on logical reasoning and symbolic AI. His belief that machines could reason using a set of rules laid the foundation for "expert systems." The challenge of giving machines "common sense," a problem he first explored, is still a major area of research today, tackled by modern technologies like large language models.
